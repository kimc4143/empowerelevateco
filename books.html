Books Worth Reading

Recommendations from Kim and the community — a mix of EA essentials and reads that expand how you think about leadership, life, and your work.

EA Essentials Beyond the Desk

EA Essentials

Required reading for executive assistants at any stage of their career

Leader
The Leader Assistant
Jeremy Burrows
A definitive guide to becoming an indispensable partner to your executive. Covers the mindset, skills, and strategies that separate great EAs from the rest.
View Book
Ultimate
Be the Ultimate Assistant
Bonnie Low-Kramen
A practical, no-nonsense guide to mastering the art of the executive assistant role — from navigating office politics to commanding the respect you deserve.
View Book
Worst
The World's Worst Assistant
Sona Movsesian
A hilarious, honest look at the EA life from Conan O'Brien's longtime assistant. Equal parts funny and surprisingly insightful about what the job actually involves.
View Book
Evolved
Evolved Assistant
Tara M. Sims
Unlocking the full potential of administrative professionals in the modern workplace. A forward-thinking look at how EAs can lead, grow, and drive real organizational impact.
View Book
Super
Super Powered
Shannon Waller
Written specifically for entrepreneurial assistants, this book reframes the EA role as a true superpower — and shows how to use it to build an extraordinary partnership with your entrepreneur.
View Book

Beyond the Desk

Reads from Kim's personal shelf — life, leadership, and a little perspective

Doing
Doing Life with Your Adult Children
Jim Burns
A thoughtful guide to navigating the evolving relationship between parents and their grown children — with grace, boundaries, and a whole lot of love.
View Book
Oak
The Women of Oak Ridge
Michelle Shocklee
A compelling historical novel set during WWII, following women who worked in secret at Oak Ridge, Tennessee — a story of sacrifice, courage, and quiet strength.
View Book
Driven
Driven
Douglas Brackmann & Randy Kelley
An exploration of what it means to be intensely driven — and how to channel that energy productively. Particularly resonant for high-performers and the people who support them.
View Book
